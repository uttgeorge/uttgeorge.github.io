Asian vs. Hispanic

Why They Are Treated Differently by Landlords

 

An eviction happens when a landlord expels people from property they own. Evictions are landlord-initiated involuntary moves that happen to renters, whereas foreclosures are involuntary moves that happen to homeowners when a bank or other lending agency repossesses a home.

And there are many reasons to be evicted by landlords, mostly because renter cannot or do not pay their rent. From The Eviction Lab report, evictions can also caused by taking on borders, damaging property, causing a disturbance, or breaking the law. In most American cities and towns, landlords can evict renters even if they have not missed a rent payment or otherwise violated their lease agreement; these are called “no fault” evictions.

That seems the common sense, what make me curious is that whether eviction rates are different in different ethnic groups? If they are different, what are the reasons?

Based on the data collected from 2000 to 2017, I plot this chart. Eviction Rate is the ratio of the number of renter-occupied households in an area that received an eviction judgment in which renters were ordered to leave. Only counts a single address per year which received an eviction judgment. The scatter plot chart reveal an interesting fact that eviction rate has positive correlation with percentage of Hispanic but negative correlation with population of Asian. In another words, when a district has more Hispanic, evictions are more likely to happen, when a district has more Asian, evictions are less likely to happen. This project is to show this fact in different chart and analyze why does this fact happen.

So I only analyze these two ethnic groups(Asian and Hispanic) both on state level and district level. Next two charts show the overall data about Asian and Hispanic. Asians are mostly in Middlesex county, and median property value and median household income of Middlesex county are higher than those of Massachusetts state. Also the poverty rate is very low.

On the other hand, Hispanics concentrate in Hampden county. the median property value and median household income of Hampden is less than median of the state. Also the poverty rate 12.81  is much higher than 4.85 of Middlesex.

Whether Hispanics are more likely to be evicted than Asians on district level? the answer is yes. The next two charts show eviction rates are higher in Hispanic districts. In Asian district, the eviction rates are very low.

On district level, Asian districts have higher household income compare with Hispanics as shown above. It is also much more expensive to buy or rent house in Asian districts than in Hispanic districts as shown below.

As shown by several charts, it is clear that Asians have more income than mass while Hispanics have less. Asians prefer live in more expensive communities but less likely to be evicted, Hispanic concentrate in those poor communities but more likely to be evicted.

So we may draw an conclusion that poverty makes poverty, and poverty causes evictions, mostly.

Make money from Apps Store

What app to make and how to price it.

(Data collection date (from API); July 2017)

If I’m a software developer, now I want to make an application to earn money from app store by selling it rather directly than from in-apps purchases. What kind of app should I make, and how to price it?

As shown above, more than half of applications in Apple apps store are games, and nearly half of games charge money.

 

The number of apps does not guarantee the number of downloading. So I choose the rating counts to estimate how many people download these apps and use them. An interesting fact reveals that despite games, people download more photo & video apps rather than education apps, though there are more education apps.

 

Can most of game apps make great profit? The answer is NO. Now multiplying rating counts by apps’ price to estimate the profit of each app. Shopping and Business apps are much more profitable than any other categories. To earn much more money, it is better to make these two kinds of apps. Shopping apps require many resources, for me, it is better to choose making business apps.

 

Since I’ve already decided what kind of apps to make, now how can I make sure customers will download it. Here are two measurements: usability and price.

There is no measurement to quantify usability in this dataset. But we can think about it from a different aspect.  People are willing to download apps whose ratings range from 4 to 5, and those being updated frequently. We can extract the keywords from those apps to understand features of them, as shown below.

PDF and Scanner are two most frequent keywords. So making business apps relating to PDF and Scanner might be useful to customers.

Then, how to price my app?

To make greatest profit, it is better to set the price as $3.99 or above $14.99. It makes sense because even the price is high, I can still earn a lot by fewer apps.
