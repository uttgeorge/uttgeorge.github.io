Make money from Apps Store

What app to make and how to price it.

(Data collection date (from API); July 2017)

If I’m a software developer, now I want to make an application to earn money from app store by selling it rather directly than from in-apps purchases. What kind of app should I make, and how to price it?

As shown above, more than half of applications in Apple apps store are games, and nearly half of games charge money.

 

The number of apps does not guarantee the number of downloading. So I choose the rating counts to estimate how many people download these apps and use them. An interesting fact reveals that despite games, people download more photo & video apps rather than education apps, though there are more education apps.

 

Can most of game apps make great profit? The answer is NO. Now multiplying rating counts by apps’ price to estimate the profit of each app. Shopping and Business apps are much more profitable than any other categories. To earn much more money, it is better to make these two kinds of apps. Shopping apps require many resources, for me, it is better to choose making business apps.

 

Since I’ve already decided what kind of apps to make, now how can I make sure customers will download it. Here are two measurements: usability and price.

There is no measurement to quantify usability in this dataset. But we can think about it from a different aspect.  People are willing to download apps whose ratings range from 4 to 5, and those being updated frequently. We can extract the keywords from those apps to understand features of them, as shown below.

PDF and Scanner are two most frequent keywords. So making business apps relating to PDF and Scanner might be useful to customers.

Then, how to price my app?

To make greatest profit, it is better to set the price as $3.99 or above $14.99. It makes sense because even the price is high, I can still earn a lot by fewer apps.
